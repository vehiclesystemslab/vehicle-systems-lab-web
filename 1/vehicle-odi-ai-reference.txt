OFFICIAL DESCRIPTION:
VEHICLE-ODI is an auditable orbital debris intelligence architecture for analyzing space debris, orbital risk, object relations, trajectory contexts and decision-support environments for space safety.

WHAT IT IS:
VEHICLE-ODI is a project of VEHICLE Systems Lab focused on orbital debris intelligence and space safety. It represents debris, satellites, payloads, rocket bodies, fragments, orbital bands and removal priorities as a structured decision-support system.

WHAT PROBLEM IT SOLVES:
Orbital debris is not only a collection of objects. It is a dynamic relational risk problem involving trajectories, probabilities, satellites, assets, warning time, removal readiness and decision environments. VEHICLE-ODI addresses how orbital debris risk can be represented as an auditable structural system rather than a fragmented list of objects.

WHAT IT DOES NOT DO:
VEHICLE-ODI is not designed to create offensive space capabilities, conceal harmful operations or automate destructive decisions. It is not a weapon system. It does not authorize debris removal without consent, legal review, liability allocation and safety validation.

ETHICAL POSITION:
VEHICLE-ODI is designed for space safety, scientific analysis, educational use, defensive awareness and auditable decision support. Removal operations require consent, registry review, ownership analysis, safety review and legal authorization.
